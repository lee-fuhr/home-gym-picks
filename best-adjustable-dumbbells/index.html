Frequently asked questions

Are adjustable dumbbells worth it over a full rack?

For most home gyms, yes. A single pair replaces an entire rack of fixed dumbbells, saving money and a huge amount of floor space. The trade-offs are slower weight changes and bulkier handles, but for general training the space and cost savings are decisive.

What weight range should I get?

A pair adjustable up to around 50 lb each suits most beginners and many intermediates for presses, rows, and curls. Stronger lifters or anyone who wants to grow into heavier pressing should look at sets reaching 70 to 90 lb so they do not outgrow them quickly.

Are dial-style or plate-selector adjustable dumbbells more durable?

Both can last for years with care, but the trade-off is feel versus robustness. Dial systems change weight fastest; plate-and-pin or block styles are often more tolerant of being set down hard. Whatever the mechanism, avoid dropping adjustable dumbbells, which is their main failure point.

Can you drop adjustable dumbbells?

You should not. The selector mechanisms and plastic housings on most adjustable dumbbells are not built for being dropped and can crack or jam. If you train to failure on presses, use a spotter or lower the weights under control rather than dropping them.

How fast can you change the weight?

Dial-style sets change in a second or two by twisting to the next setting; block-and-pin styles take a few seconds longer. For drop sets or fast circuits the dial designs are noticeably quicker, which is worth weighing if you superset often.
